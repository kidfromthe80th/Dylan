Know the Roots

Hip hop, as a cultural movement, began in the Bronx, New York City, during the early 1970s. It was a revolutionary and expressive response to the urban hardships faced by African American, Latino, and Caribbean communities.

One of the key figures in the creation of hip hop was DJ Kool Herc, who is credited with pioneering the genre's distinctive use of breakbeats. He would use two turntables to extend instrumental breaks, allowing dancers, known as breakdancers or b-boys and b-girls, to showcase their moves.

The four fundamental elements of hip hop culture are:

DJing

The art of manipulating sounds and creating music using turntables and a DJ mixer.

MCing

Also known as rapping, it is a rhythmic vocal style that incorporates rhyming speech.

Breakdancing

A dynamic style of dance that includes a variety of moves such as spins, flips, and intricate footwork.

Graffiti

A visual art form that involves creating images or writing on public surfaces, often used as a form of expression or protest.
